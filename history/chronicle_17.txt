# The Seventeenth Day of the Ashen Dreaming

The Admiral of Tidefall opened his study door at dawn, and the world did not end — which surprised, in some quiet and unspoken way, everyone who saw his face. He was pale as river clay, dry-lipped, and dressed in yesterday's coat, and he walked past the prophet waiting in the corridor as though the man were carved from the same stone as the wall behind him. No word passed between them. The Admiral moved through the palace's morning dark with the particular stillness of a man who has already made a terrible calculation and does not intend to revisit it, and when he sat at the head of the council chamber table he said only: *recall the unit.* The councillors exchanged no glances. The order was written and the dispatch rider gone within the hour, riding south toward the Dur Khadur camp four miles from the harbor where the Twin Cities watched and Caeris Thorne burned his third draft of a proclamation he could not yet afford to speak aloud. The recall would take two days to arrive. In those two days, the camp remained real, the garrison of the Twin Cities remained at elevated readiness, and Caeris's second letter — shorter than the first, colder, invoking the Hollow Accord by name — rode north toward a man who had retired to his private chamber and waved off a prophet like a man swatting smoke.

Far to the north, in the sealed garrison of Lostfeld, Thane Ironmaul stood outside the door of the room where Grand Druid Varek kept his vigil over the iron case and asked the only question that mattered anymore: *is it still sealed?* Varek answered without turning his altered eyes from the case. *Then we are still before the moment.* The Thane did not know what the moment was. He suspected that not knowing was the only mercy left to him. He rotated his most trusted veterans to the positions nearest the case and walked to the outer gate slot and told the Monastery's instruction rider, through cold iron, that no visitors would be admitted until the internal situation was resolved — a phrase that satisfied no one, least of all Ironmaul himself. What he did not yet know was that Elder Sondra Ashveil had already departed Stonebreak at dawn with two druid wardens at her back and full override authority sealed in the document case at her belt, riding north at a pace that would bring her to his gate in two days' time. She did not know Varek's full condition. She did not know what the moment was either. In the Vilefin market post on the edge of the Rock Plains, a tinker-informant had already sold what she needed to know to the highest bidder — and the highest bidder was not the Monastery. The Gloomspire Gnomes paid twenty gold before noon, received the full package, and were already preparing to fragment and resell it by the time the Monastery's contact arrived with a slower offer and an empty hand.

On the open sea off the coast of the Sinking Island, Rowen had not slept. He had broken the bronze tube's corroded seal before dawn, alone in his cabin with a knife and the patience of a man who has followed a document across the breadth of the world, and what he found inside had kept him awake through the grey hours until light came and he called the navigator back. A vellum strip. An iron key. A coordinate that described a chamber forty feet below the depth his crew had already dived, in water too dark and too cold for unprepared men. He held the key in his hand while the ship's carpenter made his assessment and the crew watched him with the particular reverence sailors reserve for captains who have not yet explained why they are anchored off a coast that seems to want to swallow them. He did not explain. He watched the carpenter and he held the key and the fleet did not move, because Rowen was a man who had come too far to sail away from an unlocked door simply because he did not yet have the right rope.
