# The World Holds Its Breath

Something old is waking beneath the world, and the world does not yet know whether to pray or run. In the mountain roots of Lostfeld, an Entity that predates every institution built above it has spent months counting upward through six hundred feet of stone, and now it has stopped counting and started *calling* — calling through the body of Kessa Stonebraid, who sleeps with bloody fingertips and wakes having written its language across the walls of her confinement room, who smiles when she hears it the way a person smiles at their own name. Thane Ulric Ironmaul has bricked the gate, posted his men, transcribed the hammering into musical notation with shaking hands, and sent increasingly desperate correspondence to a Grand Druid who is only now discovering that the pre-Accord language he has been studying in a sealed chamber — read nightly beside a preserved figure that should not still be sitting upright — is the same language rising through the stone. The warning rider is already on the high road. The junior scholars have already spoken the syllables aloud. Whether Ironmaul's sealed stone will hold anything, or whether it is simply the gesture of a man who needs to feel he has done something before the inevitable arrives, is a question the mountain has not yet chosen to answer.

Above ground, the world is fracturing along lines that were drawn long before any living sovereign understood what they meant. In the Twin Cities, King Roderic Thorne II has not emerged from a sealed chamber, and the scratch of something being signed within is the only sound his court has left to govern by, while his brother Caeris stands vigil at the door knowing nothing of the Seer walking toward him through the mountain passes, nothing of the two lords who have already signed their succession preference over to Tidefall's Admiral in a torchlit meeting that will have no ledger entry and every consequence. The Admiral himself stands on a flagship deck beneath crimson lightning he has named divine mandate, entertaining Dur Khadur mercenary language in his lower cabin, moving with the unhurried confidence of a man who has already decided the harbor belongs to him and is simply waiting for the paperwork to catch up. The Vampires slip through all of it like water — leaning close in private corridors, speaking softly to frightened lords, asking for very little now and remembering everything later. And somewhere on the Frostvale ridge, Sovereign Elowen Silverleaf has turned her war-bands away from the Shadow Court and set them watching Lostfeld instead, acting on something the Arkenor lieutenant told her in a tent with the canvas gap open — something that changed her face not with fear but with the expression of a woman who has just learned she has been fighting the wrong war.

What the world is asking, in this twenty-sixth day of the Ashen Dreaming, is the question it has always deferred until it became unavoidable: *who decides what happens when something older than every agreement, every throne, every accumulated human and elven and dwarven institution, decides it is done waiting?* Pirate King Rowen sits above a submerged iron door with a key against his palm and two days before the island takes it below safe depth forever, holding knowledge about what lies beneath the Tidefall lanes that no one above the waterline is equipped to govern. Kessa Stonebraid's palms carry the same symbols as her neck now, and the gate Ironmaul bricked shut breathes warmth through fresh mortar into the winter air, patient and utterly unhurried.
