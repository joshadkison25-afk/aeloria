ERESTERON — Duchy of Eresteron (House Braafhart) — Fertile plains / agricultural heartland — Lord Nuhoom Braafhart
Eresteron is the backbone of the High Kingdom’s survival.
Vast farmlands, disciplined rural communities, and a culture rooted in duty have made Eresteron the primary food supplier to the Twin Cities and surrounding regions. Its people are loyal—but not blind.
They remember when they were independent. And they remember that they feed the kingdom.
Eresteron supported the unification during the Orc Wars and has remained one of House Aurand’s most reliable allies—but growing taxation, political neglect, and the excess of the western merchants have begun to erode that loyalty.
They do not seek rebellion.
But they are beginning to question why they serve.

Primary Species: Humans
Eresteron’s population is almost entirely human, with a strong emphasis on lineage, family honor, and land ownership. Generations remain tied to the same soil, creating deep-rooted identity and stability.

House Braafhart (Ruling House)
A noble line built on honor, duty, and agricultural stewardship. They are loyal to the Crown—but increasingly frustrated by its weakness and dependence.

FROSTVALE — The Wintermark of Frostvale (House Adkison) - Frozen highlands / glacial mountain passes — High Lord Kaelen Adkison

Long before the rise of the Twin Cities, Frostvale’s warriors marched south as conquerors, not defenders. Their banners once stood at the edge of every major conflict in Aeloria. Now the Wintermark stands alone. Not because they fell— but because the world grew softer. Frostvale did not follow.

Primary Species: Humans
